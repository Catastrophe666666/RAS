By I. V. GILLIS
While engaged upon an investigation of the relation-
ship between the arrangement of characters as given
under the 214 radicals of the K'ang Hsi dictionary and
the 540 classifiers of the Shuo Wên dictionary, my
attention was attracted to an article written by the
Reverend Frank H. Chalfant entitled "Early Chinese
Writing," and published as Vol. IV No. 1 Memoirs of the
Carnegie Museum.
The Second section of this article consists of "Notes
upon the Shuo Wên", and a casual perusal of them and
the accompanying plates revealed so many unmistakable
errors that I was led to make a detailed review of
Chalfant's notes with the object of checking them up, and
the results of this review are set forth below.
