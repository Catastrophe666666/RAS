Contribution to the
Nomenclature of Chinese Plants.
By the late Dr. Ernst Faber.
The identification of Chinese plants by their Chinese
names is a matter of no small importance. The conflicting
names given to the same plant or fruit in different parts of
China, or even in adjacent districts, makes it difficult to
ascertain with any degree of certainty what plants are meant
by any given Chinese name. Dr. E. Faber undertook this
work and endeavoured to get the local names of plants, which
he could identify with the plants mentioned or described in
Chinese books. The executor of Dr. Faber's will handed the
list of Chinese plants given below to Dr. S. P. Barchet with
a view to its publication, and Dr. Barchet has
gone carefully
through it. It has been arranged by the Editor of the Journal
in an alphabetical order so as to facilitate the finding of the
names. The plants mentioned were classified under their
orders by Mr. D. Macgregor, Superintendent of Parks and
Open Spaces, Shanghai Municipal Council. The classification
is placed first, the alphabetical list follows and then there is
given a list of Chinese names of plants which Dr. Faber had
not been able to identify. It is to be hoped that this work of
identification may be continued by others. Dr. Faber's list
of names was taken by him from the Chinese Book
first brought out in the seventeenth century and again in
several later editions.
