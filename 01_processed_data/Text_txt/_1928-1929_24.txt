A LIST OF PLANT NAMES INCLUDED IN
THE COWDRY CHIHLI FLORA¹ NOT
RECORDED IN THE INDEX FLORAE
SINENSIS AND ITS TWO SUPPLEMENTS
By J. C. LIU, M.S.
Workers on Chinese flora rely chiefly upon Forbes
and Hemsley's Index Flora Sinensis (3) and its two
supplements (2.9) as the most complete list published
on Chinese plants up to 1910. Mr. N. H. Cowdry came
to Peking in 1919, when for several years he made
extensive travels and collections in the Western Hills,
the Eastern Tombs, Kalgan, Chefoo and many other
centres. The plants gathered were taken to America for
identification and were carefully studied and labelled by
him. These identifications were confirmed by various
authorities at Kew and elsewhere. Dr. Vincent Cowdry
after his father's death, very graciously presented these
other collections to the Department of Pharmacology of
the Peking Union Medical College. The cataloguing,
arranging in orders, mounting and putting together in
herbarium shape has been undertaken, and further
extensive collections made in Chihli province by other
workers. While working on the recently published list
of the Cowdry Chihli Flora, a number of plant names
were found not recorded in the Index Flora Sinensis
and its two supplements. Later investigation showed
that some of the plants had been discovered and named
since the publication of Dunn's Supplement (2); that
some were enumerated under different names or
synonyms; and that some could only be referred in part
to some species in the three lists mentioned above. All
such names, with different markings, are listed below.
For those plants that have not been newly discovered and
named since 1911 the corresponding names or synonyms
will be given.
