LOOKING AT A PAINTING BY WANG-TZŬ
(A T'ang Poem)
"Strange! Spend ten days to paint a mountain
Then maybe ten a glade to make."
Oh! wonder not, for the heav'n-born artist
Works only for his loved Art's sake.
What matters time to immortal Wang-tzŭ?
Perfection only is at stake.
Delightful view from a lofty hill-top
Over the lakes and the country-side!
There the village Pao-lin nestles;
Here lake Tong-t'ing stretches wide.
Clouds above like flying dragons
On the ambient azure ride.
There a fisherman in a hurry
Turns his boat to reach the shore;
It is as if a wind arises;
The foam flecked waves seem more and more.
Black clouds threaten at the horizon
The scene that was so calm before.
Oh, wondrous work! Oh, great illusion!
The eye can peer in the far-away,
A thousand li seen as ten, twelve inches;
Do cut me off a small piece, I pray;
The Sung and Wu kingdoms may suffice me,
And a few miles of the Yang-Tze, eh?
Translated by
ELFRIDA HUDSON
