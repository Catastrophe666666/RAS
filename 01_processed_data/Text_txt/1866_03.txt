WHEN I asked the western man about his name, his country,
native place and parentage, he replied "My name is Juan Bap-
tista Shidotti, and "I am from Pateirmo (?) of Rome."
Note. I could not pronounce all the words of his language,
but I have here given an approximation to his name. It was
Yowán or Oán, or Giowan. I have done the same in other cases.
Yowán is a Latin word. In Portuguese it is Joan. In Dutch
Yoán. Pateirmot is the name of a place belonging to Rome.
"My father's name was Juan Shidotti. He has been dead eleven
"years. My mother's name is Eleonora, and if she is still alive
"she is sixty-five years old."
Note. His father's name and his own are much alike, only
that he has the additional name Baptista. He told me that among
the twelve disciples of Jesus, there was a John, and that Christians
generally adopt the name of some of the apostles, whose doctrines
they receive and add it to their own. John Baptista was his name
and Shidotti his surname.
"There were four children in my fathers family, the eldest of
"whom was a daughter who died young. The next was a son
"who died at eleven years of age. This was twenty years ago.
"From my youth I received the religion of the Lord of Heaven
"pursued my studies for 22 years under 16 teachers."
